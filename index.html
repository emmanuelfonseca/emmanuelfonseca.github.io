Emmanuel Fonseca

Hello / hola / salut! I work as a radio astronomer, using and building radio telescopes to observe to most extreme objects and environments in the Universe. I am a tenure-track assistant professor in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at West Virginia University (WVU).

My specific area of research is radio-transient astronomy, with particular focus on radio pulsars and fast radio bursts. Before WVU, I was a proud member of the radio-astronomy group led by Dr. Victoria Kaspi as a postdoctoral researcher, and the group led by Dr. Ingrid Stairs during my graduate studies.
